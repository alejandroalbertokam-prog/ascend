01 — What is Ascend?

A Program Built to Elevate

Where drone technology meets human potential. We don't just teach — we transform.

ASCEND is an educational and technological program that empowers middle school and high school students to develop the soft skills that define tomorrow's leaders.

Using drone construction as a learning vehicle, students engage in real collaborative challenges that develop leadership, team management, creativity and innovation — the exact competencies that companies globally seek beyond academic grades.

Our methodology — rooted in Learning by Doing — bridges the gap between classroom theory and real-world professional demands, giving students a genuine competitive advantage.

03 — The Program

3 Modules. One Transformation.

Each session is 1.5 hours and scheduled across different days, fully adaptable to your school's calendar.

01

Foundation &
Team Setup

⏱ 1.5 Hours  |  Session 1

The first session lays the intellectual and social foundation. Students are introduced to the world of drones — their engineering, vocabulary, and possibilities — but more importantly, they begin building their team identity.

We teach the theory behind team roles (Belbin methodology), horizontal leadership, and project management frameworks like the Canvas Model — all in English to develop language and professional skills simultaneously.

  • Basic drone & engineering vocabulary in English
  • Introduction to Belbin team roles (팀 역할 이론)
  • Horizontal leadership theory (수평적 리더십)
  • Team formation and role assignment
  • Project Canvas methodology overview

02

Build,
Lead & Fail.

⏱ 1.5 Hours  |  Session 2

Theory meets practice. Students receive a drone kit and must work as a team to assemble and prepare it. This session is where real leadership dynamics emerge — under time pressure and real technical challenges.

We introduce controlled failures — deliberate technical obstacles that force teams to manage conflict, restructure their approach, and discover genuine team leadership patterns through Learning by Doing.

  • Hands-on drone assembly & engineering basics
  • Real-time role and leadership application
  • Conflict management under pressure (갈등 관리)
  • Problem solving & creative innovation (창의적 혁신)
  • Group debrief and leadership reflection

03

Compete,
Celebrate & Grow.

⏱ 1.5 Hours  |  Session 3

The culminating experience. Teams compete in a drone obstacle race — a high-energy, team-driven competition where every skill learned comes together in a tangible, exciting result.

This session channels healthy competition (건전한 경쟁) and collective ambition. Students see the direct connection between their teamwork, leadership quality, and performance — creating a memorable experience that solidifies lifelong skills.

  • Drone obstacle race by teams
  • Healthy competition & ambition (건전한 경쟁심)
  • Full integration of learned soft skills
  • Final team performance debrief
  • Certificates of participation & achievement

04 — Student Benefits

What Students Take Away

Four transformative outcomes that prepare students for the demands of the modern global workforce.

BENEFIT 01
🌐

English Proficiency

영어 의사소통 능력

All three sessions are conducted in English using technical drone and engineering vocabulary. Students develop professional communication skills in an international language — a critical edge in South Korea's globally connected job market.

BENEFIT 02
⚙️

Engineering & Assembly Skills

공학 및 조립 역량

Hands-on drone construction provides foundational exposure to hardware engineering, electronics assembly, and problem-solving under technical constraints. Students develop a tech-fluent mindset aligned with Korea's fastest-growing industry sector.

BENEFIT 03
🎯

Workplace-Ready Soft Skills

직장에서 요구되는 소프트 스킬

Students develop and apply leadership (리더십), team management (팀 관리), creativity (창의성), and innovation (혁신) — the soft skills that Korean and global employers consistently rate as their top hiring priorities beyond academic grades.

BENEFIT 04
🏅

Validated Leadership Experience

검증된 리더십 경험

Participants leave with more than a certificate — they carry a genuine, lived experience of leading a technical team through complex challenges. This shift from "seniority-based" to "performance-based" collaboration is transformative for Korean students entering the modern workforce.
